# Banking Customer Churn & Profitability Analytics — Business Insights

## Executive Summary

This project analyzed synthetic banking customer data to understand customer churn, profitability, product usage, account balances, digital engagement, support activity, and retention opportunities.

The analysis simulates how a bank or financial services company can use customer-level data to identify profitable customer segments, understand churn risk drivers, and prioritize retention actions.

## Key Findings

- Customer profitability varies across customer segments, with some segments contributing stronger balances, revenue, and monthly profit.
- Customers with fewer banking products showed higher churn risk, which suggests that product depth is an important retention factor.
- Customers with lower digital engagement were more likely to show higher churn risk.
- Customers with more support calls often showed higher churn risk, which may indicate service friction or unresolved customer issues.
- High churn risk customers with strong balances or monthly profit represent important retention opportunities.
- Product ownership, tenure, engagement, support activity, and profitability are useful indicators for churn analysis.
- The basic machine learning model helped identify which customer attributes and behaviors were most useful for predicting churn.

## Business Recommendations

- Prioritize retention outreach for high-value customers who are marked as high churn risk.
- Increase product engagement by targeting low-product customers with relevant cross-sell offers.
- Improve customer service follow-up for customers with repeated support calls.
- Encourage digital banking usage among low-engagement customers.
- Monitor churn risk by segment, product count, tenure, and support activity.
- Use churn scoring as an early warning system for customer retention teams.
- Build recurring executive dashboards to track profitability, churn risk, and retention performance.
